I. THE LOVER AND THE BELOVED vv. 1a, 7a

A. The Address
2 Tim. 3:2 “…to the angel of the church of Ephesus” (v.1a)
“…the Spirit says to the churches” (v.7a)

Angel
- messenger, guardian or representative
- most likely an elder or pastor of the local church
Church of Ephesus
- actual local church at Ephesus ( Background: Acts 18:18-21; 19)
- ekklesia - people who have been called out (of sin and the world) = believers in Christ

Ephesus
- capital of Asia Minor and is one of the influential cities in the eastern part of the Roman Empire. The temple of Artemis was located in this city.

Church (Universal Body of Christ)
- the Bride of Christ ( 2 Cor. 11:2; Eph. 5:25-27; Rev. 19:7), the Beloved

B. The Source - Jesus Christ (v.1b)
1. He is Sovereign and is our Sustainer
“…holds the seven stars in his right hand” (cf Rev. 1:20 - angels)
2. He is Present and is our Companion
“…walks among the seven golden lampstands” (cf Rev. 1:20 – churches) More than that, He is our Lover, the Bridegroom.

II. THE LOVER’S COMMENDATION VV. 2-3, 6

A. Their Deeds or Good Works -v.2a
B. Their Hard Work- v. 2a
- “toil resulting in weariness, troubles”
C. Perseverance – vv. 2a,3
- the ability to progress inspite of adversity
D. Endurance – v.3
- the ability to stand up under adversity
E. Non-compromising Stance – vv. 2b, 6
“…You cannot tolerate wicked people (false apostles)…” -v. 2b (cf Acts 20:29-31)
“…You hate the practices of the Nicolaitans…” – v. 6

Nicolaitans
- seem to have advocated compromise with the power of paganism for the Christian under pressure (New Bible Dictionary)

III. THE LOVER’S COMPLAINT v.4

A. The Complaint: Waning Love
NIV – “…you have forsaken the love you have at first”.
KJV – “…thou has left thy first love”.
NLT – “…You don’t love me or each other as you did at first!”.

Could be that God is not anymore first in their lives or their hearts have grown cold. What is most important to God: Our Heart, our Love.

B. Possible Reasons
1. Busyness with other things (even ministry) - distracted like Martha
2. Trials - sometimes causes us to doubt God’s goodness and love
3. Increase of knowledge to refute error “In the first steps of your Christian life, you may have had enthusiasm without knowledge.

Do you now have knowledge without enthusiasm?

Both are necessary if we are to keep our love for God intense and untarnished…” (NLT)
There is a difference between knowing about God and knowing God.

IV. THE LOVER’S COUNSEL vv. 5b, 7b
- given out of love (Heb. 12:5,6)

A. Remember – v. 5a
- your early days as a Christian cf. Eph. 1:15; Heb. 10:32
KJV – “Remember therefore from whence thou art fallen…”
NIV– “Consider how far you have fallen!...”

B. Repent – v. 5a
- “to change one’s mind or purpose”
- of coldness of heart and what actions caused it
C. Revive – v. 5a
- “do the things you did at first”
- Go back to the basics: Praise & Worship (Love Songs to the Lord), Prayer, Word, Fellowship, Witnessing

D. Take Heed of Two Things – vv.5b, 7b

1. A Warning
- “If you do not repent, I will come to you and remove your lampstand from its place.” (v. 5b)
- loss of influence; would cease to be an effective church
2. A Promise
- “To the one who overcomes, I will give the right to eat from the tree of life, which is in the paradise of God.” (v. 7b)
- probably set against the background of the sacred date-palm of Artemis which figures on Ephesian coins (NBD)

V. Application

What is the condition of your heart? Are you still very much in love with Jesus?
If not, heed your Lover’s counsel.
