Question: What is it in us that God is really concerned about?

Answer: Our heart

I. THE SENSE (MEANING) OF THE WORD “HEART”

A. Old Testament: Leb or Lebab
“the inner being of man; the seat of desire, inclination, or will; seat of emotions, knowledge and wisdom (mind), of conscience and moral character”
B. New Testament: Kardia
–“man’s entire mental and moral activity, both the rational and the emotional elements; contains the hidden man, the real man; represents the true character”


Eng. Cardiac;
HEART-the chief organ of physical life
One of my favorite “heart” stories: David Livingstone

II. THE STATE OF OUR HEARTS

A. Sinful and Corrupt
“The Lord saw how great the wickedness of the human race had become on the earth, and that every inclination of the thoughts of the human heart was only evil all the time (Genesis 6:5).”

“For it is from within, out of a person’s heart, that evil thoughts come—sexual immorality, theft, murder, adultery, greed, malice, deceit, lewdness, envy, slander, arrogance and folly. All these evils come from inside and defile a person (Mark 7:21-23).”

B. Deceitful and Sick
“The heart is deceitful above all things and beyond cure… (Jeremiah 17:9)”

III. THE SIGNIFICANCE OF THE HEART

A. The heart is where God looks at
“But the Lord said to Samuel, “Do not consider his appearance or his height, for I have rejected him. The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart (1 Samuel 16:7).”

B. The heart reflects who we really are
“For as he thinks in his heart, so is he (Proverbs 23:7-NKJV).”
C. The heart determines our words, decisions and actions
“For out of the abundance of the heart his mouth speaks (Luke 6:45).”
“Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it (Proverbs 4:23).”
D. The heart is the seat of faith
“For it is with your heart that you believe and are justified… (Romans 10:10).”
“Truly I tell you, if anyone says to this mountain, ‘Go, throw yourself into the sea,’ and does not doubt in their heart but believes that what they say will happen, it will be done for them (Mark 11:23).”

IV. THE SECRET TO THE HEART’S TRANSFORMATION

A.The promise of a new heart
“I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit in you; I will remove from you your heart of stone and give you a heart of flesh. And I will put my Spirit in you and move you to follow my decrees and be careful to keep my laws (Ezekiel 36:26-27).”
B.The fulfilment of the promise in Jesus Christ
“Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here! (2 Corinthians 5:17).”
- through repentance and faith in the Lord Jesus Christ

V. THE SAFEGUARDING OF THE HEART
“Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it (Proverbs 4:23).”
A.By asking God to continually search and reveal the condition of our hearts
“Search me, God, and know my heart; test me and know my anxious thoughts. See if there is any offensive way in me, and lead me in the way everlasting (Psalm 139:23,24).”

Sometimes God uses trials and tests to reveal what’s really in our hearts. EX. the Israelites, Abraham

“Remember how the Lord your God led you all the way in the wilderness these forty years, to humble and test you in order to know what was in your heart, whether or not you would keep his commands (Deuteronomy 8:2).”
In the process of shaping the clay, there is a step called “Opening Up”

B. By confessing our sins and asking God to cleanse our hearts and renew our spirit
“If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness (1 John 1:9).”
“Create in me a pure heart, O God, and renew a steadfast spirit within me (Psalm 51:10).”
C. By allowing God’s Word to dwell in us richly to keep us from sin
“Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly… (Colossians 3:16)”.

“How can a young person stay on the path of purity? By living according to your word…
I have hidden your word in my heart that I might not sin against you (Psalms 119:9,11)”.
Practice the various disciplines in the Word

VI. APPLICATION


“When was the last time we asked God to search our heart? (I hope it was this morning). Let us allow the Lord perform heart surgery in us.
Do you already have a clean and a new heart? If not, you can ask God to cleanse your heart from sin and give you a new heart.
Invite Jesus into your heart and let Him change it.
