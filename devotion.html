Luke 10:27

Sumagot ang lalaki, “Ibigin mo ang Panginoon mong Diyos nang buong puso, buong kaluluwa, buong lakas, at buong pag-iisip;’ at ‘Ibigin mo ang iyong kapwa gaya ng pag-ibig mo sa iyong sarili.’’

I. TWO KINDS OF SELF-LOVE

A. Wrong Kind of Self-Love (sinful)
2 Tim. 3:2 – self-worship; too much pre-occupation with ourselves or egocentricity

B. Right Kind of Self-Love
Luke 10:27b “...‘Ibigin mo ang iyong kapwa gaya ng pag-ibig mo sa iyong sarili.’’

II. REASONS WHY WE NEED TO LOVE OURSELVES PROPERLY

There is no direct command in the Bible that we are to love ourselves.
Why? Because it is natural (Eph. 5:28,29,33).

But there is a command to esteem ourselves properly (Rom. 12:3)

For by the grace given me I say to every one of you: Do not think of yourself more highly than you ought rather think of yourself with sober judgment, in accordance with the faith God has distributed to each of you.


A. It is the foundation of Christian love
“You are able to give to others only when you have a proper and healthy opinion of yourself.”(D.S.)

B. More importantly, it is because God loves us
(Jer. 31:3; Psalm 8:4)

III. HOW CAN WE HAVE PROPER SELF-LOVE

A. Have a Godly Self-Image and Self-Esteem

1. Definition of Self-image
- the unconscious and conscious feelings you have about yourself (C.O.)
- based on a whole system of pictures and feelings you have put together about yourself (D.S.)

2. Development of Self-Image
“The ideas of ourselves which our parents and other earthly authority figures gave us have a great deal to do with our self-image…but it was not simply what they did but how (one) reacted to them that determined (one’s self-image) (C.O.)”

3. Steps to Having a Godly Self-Image
a. Know your True Identity from God’s Word

3.1. You are God’s Special Creation

Gen. 1:27,31 – created in His image; created with a moral faculty, power of abstract thought and spiritual nature
Ps. 139:14 – created uniquely and wonderfully


Gen. 1:28; Isaiah 43:7; 1 Pet. 4:10-11 – created with a purpose: to rule over God’s creation and give Him glory through fulfilling the special work He has designed for you but most of all to know Him and enjoy fellowship with Him

3.2. You are God’s Masterful Recreation

Eph. 2:10 – God’s workmanship (from where we derive the word “poem”; masterpiece), created in Christ Jesus to do good works
We are being re-created into the very likeness of Jesus Christ (Rom. 8:28, 29)

So there is no “universal ideal” in outward beauty or appearance but in inward beauty or character (1 Sam. 16:7; 1 Peter 3:4).

Keep yourself from the unhealthy practice of comparing yourself with others. Compare yourself with Christ.
Moses: changed from being a fearful, insecure person into a confident but meek person (Numbers 12:3).

b. Know Your True Worth in God’s Eyes
You are PRICELESS (valuable, important, precious).
- more valuable than the world (Mark 8:36)
- so priceless that it cost God His own precious Son to redeem you (1 Pet. 1:15,16)

B. Have a Godly Self-Acceptance and Self-Respect

1. Appreciate your strengths and thankfully accept your weaknesses
a. Strengths – abilities and gifts (Rom. 12:6-8; 1 Cor. 12:4-11; 1 Pet. 4:10-11)
You need to discover, use (to serve others) and develop your spiritual gifts.
b. Weaknesses– inabilities; not sinful; do not see them as spiritual handicaps (2 Cor. 12:9)

If God accepts you (Rom. 15:7), who are you not to accept ourselves?

2. Be engaged in a spiritual growth process
This growth occurs as you allow God to shape your personal life through trials, making you even more capable of accomplishing future assignments (Rom. 5:2-4)

Constantly seek victory over your personal sins. Overcome sin with prayer, the Word and planned behavior, not giving in to discouragement or self-punishment.

Moses encountered many trials that developed his faith in God. He had struggles with self-control. But he had constant communion with God and growing knowledge of His Word.

If God is patiently working in you, who are you to easily give up on yourself?

3. Continually forgive yourself of ever sin, mistake or failure (Rom. 8:1,34; 1 John 3:19-21)

If God forgives you (1 Jn. 1:9), who are you not to forgive yourself?

4. Do take care of your legitimate needs (not wants).
Moses: accepted Jethro’s advice to delegate (Exo. 18:13-26).
Jesus and his disciples: Mark 6:31

If God cares for you, who are you not to care for yourself?


IV. APPLICATION

Do you already know you’re true identity not only as a person but as a redeemed person?
What new thought have you learned about yourself that you need to constantly remind yourself of?
What steps can you take to grow in self-acceptance?
